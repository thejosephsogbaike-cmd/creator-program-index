## What Practitioners Actually Do

B2B creator contracts spend most of their negotiation cycles on IP ownership and exclusivity — two clauses that rarely determine whether the relationship succeeds or fails. The clauses that do determine outcomes — content approval rights, revision process, publication timeline, termination for cause, and the handling of brand safety incidents — receive comparatively little attention until they become problems.

Part of this is structural: legal teams are trained to protect IP and limit competition. Part of it is the power dynamic in early B2B creator relationships, where the brand is often grateful for the creator's participation and reluctant to introduce friction. The practitioners who have managed the most complex creator relationships understand that the contract conversation is where you surface the expectations you both need to hold each other to — and that skipping it saves time once, at the cost of creating problems repeatedly.
