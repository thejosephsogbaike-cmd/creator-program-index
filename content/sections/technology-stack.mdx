## What Practitioners Actually Do

The B2B creator program tool stack is, in most programmes, a collection of tools that were built for adjacent use cases and adapted by practitioners who needed something now and could not wait for purpose-built solutions. Creator management platforms designed for consumer influencer programs. CRMs repurposed to track creator relationships. Spreadsheets that started as temporary and became permanent. Payment platforms that handle the transaction but not the compliance documentation.

The honest picture is that no single tool does what B2B creator program management actually requires. The practitioners who run the most operationally efficient programs have usually built a stack of three to five tools with clearly defined roles, integrated where integration is possible, and accepted the manual handoffs where it is not. They have also accepted that the spreadsheet — for roster management, content calendars, and payment tracking — is not a sign of operational immaturity. It is the most reliable tool they have.
