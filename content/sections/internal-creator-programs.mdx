## What Practitioners Actually Do

The internal creator program — where a company's own executives, founders, or subject matter experts become consistent content creators — has become one of the most requested and most underdelivered initiatives in B2B marketing. The demand is real: buyers trust people more than brands, and an accessible, credible founder or executive voice compounds in ways that paid reach does not.

The failure pattern is almost always the same. The executive commits. The first few pieces of content are produced with significant support from marketing. The content performs well. The support structure is quietly deprioritised. The executive, without production support, stops posting within six to ten weeks. The initiative is described internally as a success that lost momentum, rather than a failure of infrastructure. The practitioners who run internal creator programs that sustain beyond the initial burst have one thing in common: a defined, lightweight production system that requires minimal time from the executive while maintaining consistent output.
