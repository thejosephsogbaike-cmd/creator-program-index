## What Practitioners Actually Do

Getting paid on time, in the right amount, via a method that works for the creator's location and entity type — this is not a glamorous part of B2B creator program management. It is, however, one of the most consequential. Creators who experience friction in payment are rarely the ones who complain loudly. They are the ones who quietly deprioritise your briefs.

The operational challenge is compounded in B2B by the gap between marketing procurement cycles and creator expectations. A consumer influencer might accept 60-day payment terms. A practitioner-creator who posts on LinkedIn three times a week about B2B SaaS has options, and will use them if the payment experience repeatedly falls short of professional. The CPMs who retain their best creators have almost always resolved the payment infrastructure problem first.
