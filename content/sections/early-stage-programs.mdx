## What Practitioners Actually Do

The majority of B2B creator programs that fail in their first year do not fail because of creator quality, content strategy, or measurement approach. They fail because the company is not willing to give the programme the leadership visibility it needs to function. A two-creator programme with the CEO sharing content and actively engaging in comments will consistently outperform a ten-creator programme where the executive team treats the content as a marketing deliverable they have no relationship with.

This is the uncomfortable truth about early-stage B2B creator programs: they are not a way for leadership to outsource thought leadership. They are a way for leadership to scale its existing credibility through practitioners who have built audiences the company has not. The lever only works if leadership pulls it.
